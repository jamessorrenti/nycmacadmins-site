The best part of every meetup is someone from the community showing what
they've built, broken, and learned. If you manage Apple devices and have a
story to tell, we want you up front.

## What makes a good talk?

If you've solved a problem, other admins are fighting the same one. Popular
topics include:

- Deployment, MDM, and migration war stories
- Security and compliance (mSCP, CIS benchmarks, audits)
- Automation — scripts, pipelines, and API tricks
- Identity, Platform SSO, and passkeys
- Open source tools you build or maintain
- "How we do it at &lt;company&gt;" — real-world setups, honest tradeoffs

First time presenting? Perfect. This is a friendly room of people who all
Google the same error messages you do. Talks usually run 20–40 minutes plus
discussion, and we'll help you shape the topic.

## What to expect

- A projector, an audience of practitioners, and good questions.
- Your talk featured on the event page and in the
  [events feed](../events/index.xml).
- No sales pitches — vendor engineers are welcome to present technical
  content, per our [code of conduct](../code-of-conduct/).

## Ready?

Email us at [info@nycmacadmins.com](mailto:info@nycmacadmins.com) with a
sentence or two about your topic, or ping an organizer in
[#newyork](https://macadmins.slack.com/archives/C3APLCT1R) — we'll find you
a month.
